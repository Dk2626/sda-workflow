AI-Assisted Service Deviation & Approval Workflow — User Guide for Telecom & Media Operators

1. Introduction

The SDA Workflow platform helps Telecom and Media operators raise, route, review, and approve operational deviation requests — such as billing credits beyond standard limits, temporary bandwidth boosts, SLA waivers, content access exceptions, and KYC deferrals — while preserving regulatory auditability.

The system is enhanced with four AI agents that assist (but never replace) human decision-makers:

  • Justification Agent — drafts compliance-friendly business justifications.
  • Risk Scoring Agent — assigns LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH risk with weighted factors.
  • Context Summary Agent — produces a one-paragraph customer brief for approvers.
  • Policy Agent — surfaces relevant internal policies and contract clauses.
Tip: Every AI output is advisory. You can edit, override, or ignore any AI suggestion. All AI invocations are logged for audit.

4. Requestor Guide REQUESTOR

As an Account/Operations/Network Manager, you raise deviation requests on behalf of customers.

4.1 Create a New Deviation

1
Click + New Deviation in the top bar.
2
Select the Deviation Type (e.g., Billing Credit Beyond Limit).
3
Pick the Customer. The system auto-loads the customer's services. Optionally select a specific service.
4
Describe the Requested Action in 1–2 sentences (e.g., "Credit $4,500 to account for the November outage compensation").
5
Enter Amount (for billing credits) or Duration (for time-bound exceptions).
6
Click ✨ Generate with AI to get an AI-drafted justification. Review and edit the text — it's your final justification, not the AI's.
7
Click Save & Submit for Approval. The system runs AI risk scoring and routes the request to the appropriate approver.
Tip: You can also click Save Draft to keep editing later. Drafts are only visible to you.

4.2 Track Your Requests

Open Deviations in the top bar. You will see only requests you created. The State column shows where the request is in the approval chain.

4.3 Provide More Information

If an approver needs clarification, the request state will become INFO_REQUESTED. Open the request, read the approver's comment, and add your reply via the comment area on the detail page.

4.4 Execute an Approved Request

Once all required approvals are received, the state becomes FINAL_APPROVED. Open the request and click Mark Executed after completing the downstream action (e.g., applying the billing credit in the billing system).

5. Approver Guide L1 / Finance / Network

You review and approve requests routed to your role.

5.1 Find Pending Requests

Open Deviations. Use the State filter to show only UNDER_REVIEW items. The Awaiting column tells you which role is responsible — your role should match for items you must act on.

5.2 Approver Workbench (Request Detail Page)

Opening a request shows a comprehensive view:

  • Risk badge — AI-assigned LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH.
  • AI Context Summary (auto-loaded) — one paragraph snapshot of customer & service.
  • Relevant Policies — click "Load" to fetch AI-cited policies.
  • AI Risk Assessment — full list of contributing factors and weights.
  • Action History — every prior approval action with comments.

5.3 Take Action

In the right-hand panel:

  1. Type a mandatory comment (minimum 5 characters).
  2. Choose your action:
    • Approve — moves to the next approver or to FINAL_APPROVED.
    • Request Info — sends it back to the requestor.
    • Reject — terminates the request.

5.4 Override the AI Risk Score

If you disagree with the AI risk band:

1
Click Override AI risk score.
2
Pick the new band (LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH).
3
Write a reason of at least 20 characters. Mandatory by policy.
4
Click Save Override. The action is recorded with your reason.
Note: Downgrading a HIGH risk to LOW or MEDIUM notifies the Compliance team and is highlighted in the audit log.

6. Compliance Officer Guide COMPLIANCE_APPROVER

You hold final-tier approval for regulatory-sensitive requests and may veto any high-risk or KYC/Content deviation.

6.1 Compliance Mandatory Cases

Deviation TypeCompliance Required?
DT_KYC_DF (KYC Deferral)Always
DT_CONTENT (Content Access)Always
DT_SLA_WV (SLA Waiver)Always
DT_BILL_CR (Billing Credit)Only when amount > USD 5,000
DT_DATA_BW (Bandwidth Boost)Only when AI flags HIGH risk

6.2 Veto Authority

You may reject a request at any point after submission, even if L1 and L2 have already approved. Use the Veto / Reject button on the detail page. The action is recorded as COMPLIANCE_VETO in the audit log.

Important: A veto is final. The state transitions to REJECTED regardless of prior approvals. Use this power judiciously and ensure your comment captures the regulatory or contractual basis.

7. Auditor Guide AUDITOR

You have read-only access to every request and its complete history.

7.1 Open the Audit Page

Click Audit in the top bar. Enter the deviation ID (visible in the URL of the detail page, e.g., /deviations/3 → ID is 3) and click Load.

7.2 What You See

  • Audit Events — chronological list of every state change, login, AI call, and override, with actor and timestamp.
  • AI Invocations — every call to an AI agent, with the prompt (truncated), response, model, tokens, duration, and confidence score.

7.3 Export

For audit-ready CSV exports of all deviations, click Download CSV from the Dashboard.

8. Administrator Guide ADMIN

9. Using AI Features Effectively

9.1 Justification Generation

  1. Fill in customer, service, action, and amount/duration before clicking the AI button — more context, better output.
  2. Review the generated text. Look for:
    • Factual accuracy (does it match what you actually requested?).
    • Tone (it should be neutral and professional, not promotional).
    • Policy alignment (does it reference any policies that don't apply?).
  3. Edit or rewrite as needed. The published justification is yours, not the AI's.

9.2 Reading the Risk Score

The risk score includes:

  • Band — LOW (green), MEDIUM (amber), HIGH (red).
  • Factors — each factor's weight (0–1) and a short value/reason.
  • Confidence — surfaced in the audit log. Confidence below 0.7 deserves extra scrutiny.

9.3 PII & Privacy

Before sending data to the AI provider, the system replaces customer names and codes with tokens such as [CUSTOMER]. You will see this in audit prompts. This protects PII while preserving structural context for the model.

11. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why can't I see another user's draft request?

By design, requestors see only their own requests. Approvers, auditors, and admins see all.

Q: Can I delete a request?

No. Audit retention rules forbid hard-deletion. Use Withdraw (before approval) or Reject (after submission).

Q: The AI says my deviation is "MEDIUM" but I think it's "HIGH". What do I do?

Use the Override AI risk score action with a written reason. Both the original AI score and your override are kept in the audit log.

Q: What happens if the AI service is down?

The system uses a synthetic fallback so the workflow continues. The audit log will show model name fallback-mock, and approvers will see lower confidence indicators.

Q: How long is data kept?

Audit log entries are retained for at least 7 years by policy (NFR-004). Deviation records and AI invocations are kept indefinitely.

Q: Can I approve my own request?

No — segregation of duties is enforced. The system blocks any approve/reject by the original requestor.
